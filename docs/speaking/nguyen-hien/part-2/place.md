### A country you want to visit

I'm going to talk about Japan, the one country that I always dream of a visiting. It is one of the most beautiful and friendly country of the east Asia region.

Japan is famous for for its natural features like the vast Ricefield or the breathtaking Fuji mountain if you visit this country in the spring, you can enjoy the flower blooming the river flowing, and bird singing. Moreover, by participating in local events and festivals - like tea, drinking ceremony or making mochi cakes you will be immersed yourself into the life of the local.

In terms of food, they are various kind of traditional dishes in Japan - including the ones that you perhaps already know, Ramen, sushi, etc. It is possible to try these in Việt Nam but it would be amazing to have a taste from the place where these food originated from.

Also, the country has four amazing season. I'm really amazed by the seasonal changes in the nature through videos and documentaries on the Internet. Each season has its own unique beauty. Oh, you can enjoy flower blooming in the spring, beautiful pictures in the summer yellow lives falling in the autumn and participating in what Christmas during the winter.

I have never visited this country ever before and would love to do it one day. Perhaps it would be a solo travel so that I can get the most out of this trip.

So if I had to talk about a country that I wish to visit in the future, it would have been Japan.

### A city you have visited



Today, I want to talk about Hanoi, the city that I recently paid a trip to.

Hanoi is the capital, and one of the major cities of Vietnam, beside HCMC. This city has a lot to offer, in terms of infrastructure, culture and occupation opportunities. I actually came to this city last Jan to attend a job interview, and also as a break to my daily life. Hanoi attracts lots of tourists thanks to its skyscrapers, many malls and night markets for shopping lovers, and its upbeat live style. Moreover, many of the int head are placed in Hanoi, so this city fosters a multicultural city, one that welscome people from all nationalities. You can also immersed yourself into the local experience, by having a stroll around the Hoan Kiem walking street, for have a cup of beer with strangers at a rooftop bar on Bui Vien western street.

In terms of food, there are a number of specialities here - I have tried bun cha (food that Obama had), nem, pho, etc. While I could have tried all of these food back in DN, but it was amazing to enjoy authentic meals in the place where those food originated from.

Climate is sadly, one of the down side of this city. Being a rapidly changing region, HN has a high level of polution - I witnessed that no one went outdoor without their face masks. This really deteriorated the otherwise amazing time that I had there.

I wish that the government can have decisive actions to this problem - in the meantime, I cannot recommend this city to anyone for leisure purposed.

So if I had to talk about my recent trip to a city, it would be Hanoi.
