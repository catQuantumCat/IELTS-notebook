#### Paragraph
The chart illustrates the number of working hours men and women in Australia spent weekly.

Overall, most Australians worked for 30-50 hours per week, with more men choosing to work for longer shifts while the opposite was true for women. The working hour categories of 0 to 10 and 10 to 20 saw more participation from female employees than their male counterparts.

In the part-time range, a higher proportion of women worked for **fewer hours** than men. Just under 5 percent of men spent less than 10 hours a week working, whereas the figure for females was tripled that, at 15 percent. Similarly, in the 10-20 hour shifts, approximately 13% of men worked in such hours, compared to 22% of females.

Considering the full-time working hours, men often worked for more time than women. The peak category for both men and women was 30-50 hour, with men accounting for 42% and women for 35%. Furthermore, this gap between two sexes was widened further considering the 60-80 working hours category, where males made up 30% while 18% of females worked for those hours. Interestingly, the proportions of men and women who worked more than 80 hours a week was identical, both at 10%.
