The table gives information about the proportion of four main housing categories in Victoria, the Northern Territory and Tasmania, between 2001 and 2006.

Overall, houses were the most popular dwelling type in all listed areas. Moreover, the percentages for this property type were higher in Tasmania than any other regions, while the opposite was true for the figures in the Northern Territory.

From 2001 to 2006, the shares of houses in Victoria and the Northern Territory dropped marginally, from 79% and 65% down to 76% and 63%, respectively. Meanwhile, Tasmania witnessed <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">a one percent point increment</mark> in its figure of housing, to 89% - the highest of all. Semi-attached houses gained slightly more interest over a five-year span, with their figures across three regions all going up around 1-2%.

Around 11% of all dwellings in the Northern Territory were flats in 2006, with that figure for Victoria being 10%. <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">The jumps represented a 3% and 1% increase over five years, respectively</mark>. Meanwhile, the proportion of apartments in Tasmania decreased negligibly; down to 6%. The 'Other' category in the Northern Territory went down to 11% after a three percentage points decrease, <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">while staying minimal</mark> in Victoria and Tasmania (2% and 1% respectively).
