The chart gives insights about the immigration, emigration, and net rates in the UK, from 1999 to 2008.

It is of note that, all figures mentioned in the graph shared an increase trend over the time period. The number of immigrants in the United Kingdom was always higher than that of emigrants, but with various gaps over the years. There were six out of ten years that the difference between these two figures was below 200 thousands, with 5 of them recorded on the first half of the time window.

In 1999, the number of people left UK for another country was about 450 thousands, while almost 300 thousands people came to live in this nation. Not until 2002 that there were any significant changes, when the rate recorded for emigrants in the UK jumped to about 375 thousands people, with that of immigrants surpassed half a million after a slight increase. The discrepancies between the net values in the first 5 years were insignificant, ranging around 150 thousands people.

In 2004, the total of immigrations was almost at 600 thousands as a result of a dramatic jumped from the prior year. Combined with the marginal fall from the emigration number, the 6th year in the time period marked the highest net value of all — at around 250 thousands people. Most people immigrated into the UK in 2006, with it's figured peaked at 600 thousands, before ending the time period with minimal changes. In contrast, figure for emigration experienced dramatic fluctuations; the number plummeted in 2007 before soaring significantly in 2008.
