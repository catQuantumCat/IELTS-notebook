This diagram gives information about how pineapples are grown and post-processed.

Overall, this linear process consists of 2 phases, beginning with pineapples being planted and ending with either the making of consumer-ready products or logistical preparation.

Initially, pineapples are placed under the sun and spaced 26 centimeters apart, with temperature being kept ~~at~~ between 28 and 30 degrees. After seven months, they are sprayed with artificial substances, then harvested five months later. At this moment, the largest fruits can reach 30 cm in height and ~~weight~~ weigh two kilograms.

In the preparing stage, pineapples are washed under fresh water and categorised based on their sizes. Small and medium ones have their ~~head~~ heads chopped and their skin ~~pealed~~ peeled to retrieve only the inner content. Next, smaller fruits are made into juice through conventional extractors, whereas the medium-sized ones are sliced into smaller pieces before being contained in tin cans. For the large pineapples, they are covered in wax, collected whole into batches, and loaded onto ships to be transported to other regions.
