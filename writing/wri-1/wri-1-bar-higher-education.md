The chart gives information about the higher education participators in five countries, categorised by sex, in 2001.

Overall, the majority of the listed nations had more men finishing higher education than women. This was exceptionally true in Switzerland, while the opposite can be said for Germany.

In 2001, almost half of male residents in Switzerland had higher education - the highest figure of all - with that of females standing at 35%. Meanwhile, those percentages in Britain were marginally lower, at 35% and 30%, respectively. The same gap can be witnessed for the proportions of citizens in Japan, with 30% men and 25% women possessing higher education qualifications.

Among countries with female dominance in higher education program completion, 45% of German women completed these curriculums, 5% higher than that of Americans. This figure was also significantly higher than that of their male peers in Germany, which was only at 35%. While the same figure was shared with the proportion of American males, the difference with that of the US females was smaller.
