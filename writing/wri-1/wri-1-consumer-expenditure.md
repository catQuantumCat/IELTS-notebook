The table shows spending on  3 consumer good categories, in 5 nations in 2002.

Overall, the majority of expenditure among 5 countries was on food, drinks and tobacco. Moreover, citizens in Turkey and Ireland spent more money on the 3 categories than those in other countries.

The proportion of budget allocated for food, drinks and cigarettes was around 32 and 29 percent in Turkey and Ireland, significantly higher than that of Italy, Sweden and Spain with figures no higher than 19%. Regarding fashion and footwear, Italians spent exactly 9% of their money on this group - the highest of all. Meanwhile, Turkey was the biggest spender in the leisure and education category - its figure stood at 4.35%.

Among the regions with lower total expenditures, people in Sweden allocated no more than 16% of their budget on food, drinks and tobacco, and around 5.5% for clothing and footwear products - the lowest of all compared to other nations. The same can be said for Spain's expenditure for leisure and education activities - inhabitants of this country spent only about 2% for this product group.
