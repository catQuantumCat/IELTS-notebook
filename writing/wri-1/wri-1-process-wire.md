This diagram gives insights into the process of producing wire.

Overall, this man-made, linear production line consists of nine steps, starting with burning a combination of raw materials, and ending with a cooling step.

At the start, coking coal and iron ore are mixed and fed into a blast furnace at 1300 degrees. Through pipes, this mixture is transported to an arc furnace (set at 1800 degrees), being burnt with an addition of air and reduced coal. After which, the output is again moved into another furnace at the same temperature, albeit with lime added and gas as a byproduct. At this stage, the initial input is now in a liquid state, ready for the next stage.

Vats of liquid metal are transported to moulds, where stripes of metal wire are given their initial shape. This form is further secured through specialized machines that shape, press and cool down the wires - the last process is done at the temperature of 150 degrees. After an additional round of cooling, sets of metal wire coil are now ready for purchase.
