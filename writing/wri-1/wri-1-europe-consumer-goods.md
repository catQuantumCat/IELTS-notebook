The chart compares the spending on six products between citizens in Germany, Italy, France and Britain.

Overall, British people spent significantly more on all six goods than any other listed European countries. Moreover, out of the six products, consumers spent the most money on photographic film, while the total expenditure recorded for personal stereos was the lowest of all.

Britain's citizens expended no less than 150 thousand pounds for each item. In particular, expenditures on CDs, toys, and film rolls even exceeded the 160-thousand-pound mark, making this country the highest spender of all. The opposite can be said for Germany, for its residents' budget for the mentioned goods was around 150 thousand pounds each - with insignificant differences between them. 

Regarding Italy and France, the two nations shared the same amount of money spent on toys - both laid out nearly 160 thousand pounds for products of this category. However, French purchasing focused more on photographic film canisters and CDs, while Italians paid out more on perfumes, tennis racquets and personal audio devices.
