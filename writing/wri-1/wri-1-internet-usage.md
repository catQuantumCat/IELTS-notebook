- Fixed:
	The graph illustrates the proportion of population that opted in to use internet services in a ten-year period, in the US, Mexico and Canada.
	
	It is of note that over a decade, the percentages of internet users experienced an increase trend, with a much larger percentage of Canadians and Americans had Internet access in comparison with Mexicans. By the end of the time period, Canadians had the highest proportion of its citizen online.
	
	In 1999, internet **penetration** in the US **stood at around** 20%. The figure for Canada and Mexican were slightly lower, at about 10% and 5%, respectively. **A notable surge** occurred in Canada between 2001 and 2002, where internet usage jumped by 15%, thus surpassing the US to become the leading nation. In the same time gap, the figure for Mexico saw an increase, but with modest growth.
	
	By 2005, Canada and the US both reach internet usage of 70%, **indicating** a similar level of **adoption**. 
	Following this, the gap between these two nation's figure widened considerably. By 2009, Canada's internet penetration **neared** 100%, compared to about 80% of Americans and 40% of Mexicans.
