The pie charts give insights into the electronic game market in South Korea in 2003, in terms of the player's age, gender and favourite type of game.

Overall, most game players in South Korea were male, while the minoriy were women and those in the above-36 age group. Moreover, action games were more popular than any other listed categories.

In 2003, just under 40% of people who played games in South Korea were children or teenagers with that of the 18-36 group being closely behind, at 38%. That also meant the remaining 23% was that of those in the 37-and-up age bracket. Regarding gender, almost three-quarters of the total headcount were male, yet the same percentage for the female counterpart accounted for 28%.

The action genre dominated the South Korean's game market, chosen by 43% of all gamers. Meanwhile, the percentages of sport and racing games were less than half of that, standing at 20% and 17%, respectively. Among the last 20% of players, with 7% of them picking either role-playing or educational games, that proportion of the other categories was even lower, only at 6%.
