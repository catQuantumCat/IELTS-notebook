The table gives information about the amount of milk produced every year in four countries, between 1990 and 2010.

Overall, the Netherlands and Australia were most significant milk-producing regions, with figures far exceeding those of Tanzania and Guatemala. However, production trends diverged; the Netherlands' figure nearly remained unchanged, Australia's declined, and Tanzania and Guatemala experienced growth.

In 1990, milk output levels in the Netherlands and Australia were similar, at 11,262,000 and 11,246,000 litres, respectively. The Netherlands's milk production dropped slightly in 2000, before making a comeback; ending the time period with nearly 11,5 million liters of milk produced. Australia's metric also had a marginal decrease in 2000, but the falling momentum continued, eventually reaching only around 9,1 million liters. 

Among the countries with lower milk volume, that of Tanzania was three times higher than Guatemala's in 1990 (87 thousand liters compared to 26 thousand liters). Over two decades,  Tanzania saw a surge in milk production; its output nearly doubled to 155 thousand liters in 2010. The jump was even more pronounced in Guatemala, whose figure stood at 84 thousand liters in 2010 - a 3-fold increase.
