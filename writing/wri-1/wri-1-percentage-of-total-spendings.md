>The charts below show the percentage of total spendings of a school in the UK in 1981, 1991 and 2001.
![[wri-1-percentage-of-total-spendings-graph.png]]


The pie charts illustrate the proportions of the overall budget allocated for various categories from 1981 to 2001 in a UK's school.

It is of note that over two decades, while the amount of money spent for resources and other professional's salaries decreased, the opposite was true for the remaining sectors. Moreover, the budget for teachers' wages was the highest spending at the end of the time period, while more than half of total expenses were distributed for other categories.

The budget proportion for non-teachers’ salaries witnessed a continuous downfall over two decades, from nearly 30% in 1981 to just 15% in 2001. On the other hand, the spending for teaching materials had slightly increased, before experiencing the same downward tendency, ending up at 9% of the total educational budget.
