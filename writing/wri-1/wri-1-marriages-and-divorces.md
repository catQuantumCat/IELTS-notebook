The first bar chart gives information about the figure of US's marriages and divorces between 1970 and 2000, while the second shows the percentages of marital status among American adults in the same time period.

Overall, the United States witnessed a fall in the number of recorded marriage cases. Moreover, while the majority of the American's adult population were married, the proportion of <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">single adults</mark> slightly increased.

In 1970, about 2,5 million marriages were recorded in the US, with the figure for divorces <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">less than half that</mark>. The number of marriages remained stable for the next 10 years, <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">before marginally decreasing</mark> down to 2 million in 2000. In contrast, more couples divorced from 1970 to 1980, bringing the figure of divorces to its peak of around 1.5 million. From this point onward, this number gradually fell down to eventually return to its initial figure - at 1 million.

Approximately 70% of American adults were married in 1970. However, this proportion dropped to just under 60% three decades later. In the same time period, the percentages of people who stayed unmarried and divorced rose by less than 10% each, reaching 20% and around 10% respectively. The ratio of widowed people to the total US adult population was at 5%, slightly lower than that in 1970.
