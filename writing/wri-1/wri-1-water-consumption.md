The graphs give insights about the global water usage from 1900 to 2000 from 3 sectors, and compare in detail the water consumption per person in Brazil and Congo in 2000

It's of note that over a century, figures for water usage worldwide experienced an increasing trend, with agriculture being the highest water consumption sector. At the end of the time period, Brazil used significantly more water when compared to Congo.

In 1900, Agricultural activities utilized around 500 km squared of water. At the same time, the differences between the amount of water consumed by industrial and domestic sectors were neglectable — both stood at around 100 km squared. In 2000, farming activities demanded 6 times more water than 100 years ago, its figure was at 3000 km2. Meanwhile, industrial sector's water usage was up ten-fold, with about 1200 km2 of water used, and domestic consumption reached approximately 500km3.

In the year 2000, the population of Brazil was 176 million, while 5,2 million of people were living in Congo at that time. The amount of water consumed per capita in Brazil and Congo were 359m3 and 8m3, respectively. As Brazil had 265 times more irrigated land than Congo, this might explain why the rate of Brazil was higher than that of Congo.
