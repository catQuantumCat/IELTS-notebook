The table compares several aspects of global fishing between two sectors.

Overall, industrial ships dominate almost all operational metrics, with higher oil consumption, catch volumes and capital invested. In contrast, smaller boats have a larger workfoce.

Every year, large-scale fishing activities require half a million of employee onboard to be carried out - 24 times lower than that of local boats (at 12 million). Despite a larger workforce, the latter sector yields slightly less annual food consumption, at 24 million, versus 29 million produced by larger ships. In addition, the annual output for industrial processing from the private sector is 22 million, yet there is almost no capture from small boats.

As there are far less people working for the private fishing sector, each position are more costly to fill, from 30 to 300 thousand dollar every year. In contrast, smaller boats only need to spend from 250 to 2500 dollars per occupation. In terms of fuel oil usage, industrial-sized ships need to utilise 14-19 million tonnes for yearly fishing, while the other industry use far less (1.5 - 1.8 million)
