The table shows data about the underground rail networks in 6 major cities.

Overall, the table gives insights about the opening date, the total railway length and yearly passenger count of each of the 6 cities. It is noticeable that the three oldest rail networks are much longer and have more passengers per year than **their** newer counterparts.

London has the oldest underground railway system, **having opened** in 1863. With 394 kilometres of route, **it is** visited by 775 million passengers every year. Opened in 1900, Paris's underground system serves a significantly larger number of visitors despite being half as long. The third oldest system belongs to Tokyo, **which** serves nearly 1930 million people yearly - the highest year-over-year passenger figure of all.

Among the three newer railway systems, Washington DC's underground is the most extensive. Opened in 1976, about 145 million passengers travel on **this 126 kilometres long route**. Those figures for Kyoto's route are 45 million and 11 kilometres - this makes **it** both the shortest and least popular underground railway system of all. The newest system - built in 2001 - belongs to Los Angeles, with a modest length of 28 kilometres and is used by 50 million people every year.
