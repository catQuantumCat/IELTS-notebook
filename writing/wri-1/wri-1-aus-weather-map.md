The diagram illustrates weather forecasts collection flow of the Australian Bureau of Meteorology to broadcast to the general public.

Overall, this process consists of 4 steps, starting by gathering raw data from various sources. This information is then dissected and prepared for presentation, before being served to the audiences.

At the first and second stages, weather information comes from three sources. Firstly, satellite clusters can capture photos from the sky, or send those to radar stations - at which the information is shown on a radar screen to be analysed. These stations can also collect data themselves, either to be shown on said screen, or to be presented as synoptic charts. The creation of these charts can also be done using the inputs intercepted by drifting buoys on the ocean.

Having received raw data from different types of data sources and going through the analysis and forecasting phase, the broadcast is edited. After which, viewers are provided with up-to-the-minute data on the weather using many communication media - through TV weather programmes, radio channels or landlines.
