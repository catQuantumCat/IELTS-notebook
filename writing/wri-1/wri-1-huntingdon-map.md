The maps show the current layout of Huntingdon, and the proposed plan for the town in the future.

Overall, Huntingdon is planned to undergo a substantial transformation, involving changes to land uses and reorganization of the transportation system. Key features of the plan being the repurpose of the airfield to be an industrial estate, and the new road infrastructures.

At present, an airfield is located on the northwestern top of the town - connected to the A1 road. In the future, this site will be removed and replaced by an industrial estate. On the west, the current built-up area - consist of houses, stores and others - will remained unchanged. The same can be said for the railway station, with new routes being made providing access to the new industrial area.

The road network is planned to have significant changes. As of now, A14 is handling traffic traveling east-west, crossing Bampton road via an overpass. This junction will be replaced by a roundabout, altering traffic movements in the area. A14 will run along the southern edge, connecting A1 with a new road that branched of Ring road.
