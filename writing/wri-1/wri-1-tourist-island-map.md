The maps show layout of an island, first in its initial state, and the changes made after the addition of tourist facilities.

Overall, the site underwent a substantial transformation, from an uninhabited island to a predominant leisure area. Key changes include the construction of tourist amenities and the paths and tracks providing access to those features.

One of the noticeable differences involves the new tourist structures. Before construction, the area was mostly empty, with trees sparingly distributed and a beach northwest of the island. Most of the area is occupied with tourist facilities - a reception on the western side, a restaurant built north of it, and a pier located on the south bank. On either side of the reception, accommodations are arranged in circular groups. Despite the extensive renovation, the existing plants and beach remain unchanged, yet the latter is made available for swimming.

All of the new properties are accessible via footpaths and roads. The paths allow for easy on-foot traveling from the swimming area to the residential clusters and back, while the tracks support transportation activities between the boating pier, reception area and restaurant.

- Features
	- Old: mostly empty/vacant areas
	- New: A main reception built west of the island, with a restaurant north of it, and a boat pier on the south edge.
	- Clusters of accommodation built on either side of the main reception, arranged in circular areas
	- The beach on the westernmost side are now open for swimming, while the eastern end of the island remains empty.
	- All the man made features are built without removing any trees
- Paths
	- footpath + roads => easier navigation on the island.
	- Footpaths allow on-feet access to all of the amenities, while the road where built providing access to the pier, reception area and the restaurant.
