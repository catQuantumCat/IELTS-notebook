The chart compares the top three makers of milk, cheese, butter, and milk powder in 2012.

Overall, the EU was the only region among the top three of all listed dairy product makers. Moreover, the milk production yielded the highest quantity, while that of milk powder was the lowest.

In 2012, the EU manufactured more than 140 thousand and seven thousand metric tons of milk and cheese, higher than any other regions. Of the former category, India and the US were the second and third largest - having produced 127 and 90 thousand metric tons, respectively. Meanwhile, the same positions for cheesemaking were held by the US and Brazil - with figures standing at nearly five thousand, and seven hundred metric tons.

The production gap was more pronounced in the making of butter. The EU's output was less than half of India's (2 thousand versus 4,5 thousand metric tons). Furthermore, the US's figure was five times lower than the biggest maker (at around eight hundred). In terms of milk power, about 1.2 metric thousand tones were originated from China, with that of New Zealand and the EU around 1,1 thousand and seven hundred.
