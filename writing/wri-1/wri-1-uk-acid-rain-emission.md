The graph gives information about the amount of acid rain pollutants — measured in millions of tones — produced by 4 distinct sectors in the UK, from 1990 to 2007.

It is of note that all industries, except for transportation and communication, witnessed a fall in their figures of acid rain emissions. Noticeably, electricity, gas, and water's acid gases declined the most in comparison to other sectors.

In 1990, the industry of electricity, gas, and water was the largest polluter, responsible for approximately 3.3 million tonnes of acid rain emission in the UK. The sectors of domestic, and transport and communication emitted roughly the same amount of acid rain, with their metrics at 0.6 and 0.7 million tonnes, respectively, whereas other industries contributed just over 2 million tonnes.

After almost two decades, the figure for electricity, gas, and water supply's acid gas pollutants plummeted by 2.8 million tonnes, down to just 0.5 million tonnes, thereby losing its position has the top emitting entity. Emissions from domestic and other industries also gradually fell, resulting in 0.3 and 1.3 million tonnes. In contrast, Transportation and communication sector experienced an initial rise in the emission amount, peaked at 1 million tones in 2005; before falling back to its 1990 figure of 0.7 million tones at the end of time period.
