The table gives information about the advertising spending of four UK car makers in 2002.

Overall, Renault <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">had a larger budget allocated for advertising than any other automobile company.</mark> Most of the expenditures were used on running ads on TV and newspapers, while the opposite was true for the other advertising categories.

In 2002, <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">television accounted for the vast majority of advertising costs for all car manufacturers. </mark>With 70 million dollars spent, Cetrizine was the largest spender on this category, which was substantially higher than the 45 million dollars outlay from Rover. Renault and Rover's investments for ads on press stood at 45 and 38 million dollars, respectively. In contrast, budgets from Cetrizine and Vauxhall were significantly less, with 12 million and 8 million used.

Expenditure on radio advertisements was the same between car makers, at 15 million dollars each. Spending on outdoor and cinema ads was minimal, with no more than 7 million dollars spent on these two categories<mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">. No expenditure was recorded</mark> for cinema advertising by either Rover or Cetrizine.
