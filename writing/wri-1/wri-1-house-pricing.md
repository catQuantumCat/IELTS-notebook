The chart shows changes of the average house prices between 5 major cities from 1990 to 2002, compared to the year 1989.

Overall, most areas in the first five years witnessed decreases in housing real-estate values, while the opposite was true for the next seven years. In particular, London's figure shifted the most, yet that of Frankfurt remained somewhat stable.

From 1990 to 1995, the average house property in London, Tokyo and New York was more affordable than that in 1989. In detail, houses in London and Tokyo were 7% cheaper, while in New York it was 5%. At the same time, residents in Madrid and Frankfurt had to allocate marginally more to own houses, with the price increases not exceeding the 3% mark.

Between 1996 and 2002, the UK's capital city saw its average house pricing skyrocketed, with the percentage jumping from -7% to 12%. A similar upward trend was observed in New York, Madrid and Tokyo, with the percentage change standing at 5%, 4.5%, and -5%, respectively. In contrast, citizens of Frankfurt - on average - were able to purchase houses at a lower price - only 2% higher than that in 1989, instead of 3% in the previous time period.
