The bar chart gives information about the number of different visitors who came to a museum - between 1997 and 2012.

Overall, it is noticeable that most of the museum goers were adults. Moreover, the highest number of visitors from this age group, and that of all guests, **was** recorded in 2007.

Between 1997 and 2002, changes to the figures across all types of visitors were minimal. In particular, the numbers recorded for adult and special visitors remained stable - at around 300 and 29 thousand, respectively. The opposite can be said for the quantity of teenagers under 15 - this age group's headcount marginally decreased by 10 thousand, to 110 thousand people. In total, this museum attracted over 440 thousand people in 2002, down from nearly 450 thousand in 1997.

In 2007, a total of 525 thousand people visited the museum. This peak was reached - largely due to the jump in the figure for grown-up guests, as it surpassed the 400 thousand mark. As this number fell to 380 thousand people in 2012, so too did the total headcount, down to 504 thousand. Meanwhile, fewer youngsters under 15 visited the museum - only around 82 thousand of them did in 2012. Yet that of special visitors kept on its incline trend, eventually reaching 42 thousand.
