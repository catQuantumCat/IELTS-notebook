The photo illustrates the stages of using coal to produce electricity.

Overall, there are seven steps in this man-made process, beginning with coal mining and pre-processing, and ending with the creation of electricity through power plants.

At the beginning, raw blocks of coal are extracted from coal mines. These are then ground into a fine powder by crushing machines, before being collected into clusters. Batches of ground coal are now ready for *logistics* - first by industrial carts, then through trucks *before arriving at* gasifier facilities.

From this point, coal is transformed into gas to be ready for the next steps - which also produces solid waste as a byproduct. Next, this gas goes through a cooling stage and *is moved* to gas power plants. At this stage, it is again converted into the final form - electric energy, serving domestic and industry power consumption needs. However, this last step is not entirely efficient, *with some energy escaping as heat waste*.
