This diagram gives insights into the creation of an igloo from snow.

Overall, this man-made, linear process consists of 8 steps - starting with the making of snow blocks and ending with filling the air gaps.

At the start of this process, snow blocks in different sizes are tightly packed and chopped into pieces using a snow saw. Two layers of the bigger ones are laid out vertically, creating a foundational area with a 4m diameter. Next up, another two layers of smaller bricks are placed on top, and along with a disk put at the peak, the initial form of an igloo is created. To strengthen the bond between these snow segments, some amount of loose snow is applied. At this stage, the exterior is completed, and should be 2m in height. 

Next up, access to the interior is created through an entrance, narrow in height and stemming from the dome. Lastly, small air holes between the bricks are filled using a special gun from the inside. After which, the construction is completed, and the igloo is ready to be used.
