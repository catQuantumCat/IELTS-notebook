The maps shows the current layout of the Norbiton industrial area, and its planned development.

Overall, the area is expected to undergo extensive redevelopment; changing from a designated industrial site to a predominant residential spacing. Key changes include the replacement of the factory cluster with houses and public amenities, and the reorganisation of the traffic infrastructure.

Currently, the site mainly consisted of factories surrounding a central roundabout, with a river running along the northern edge of town, and a farmland beyond it. In the proposed plan, a new road will extend from the roundabout, crossing the river and providing access to a new residential area, reducing the size of the surrounding farmland. In addition, there will be an another roundabout to improve the connection to the main east-west road.

On the eastern side, the existing road will be retained and extended to serve the newly built school, and a playground north of it. A medical centre is also planned to the southeast of the central roundabout. To the west, shops will be added in the southwestern edge, while further housing will be developed along a new road branching westward from the roundabout.
