People believe that spending time outdoors is more beneficial to children's growth than playing video games. While games are incredibly attractive to youngsters, I totally agree that outdoor sports and leisure activities are more advantageous to a kid's development - both mentally and physically. 

To start, children playing video games excessively can lead to substantial consequences. Every hour a child spends getting lost into games' fantasy worlds is an hour their body is not in active motion. This greatly hinders their physical growth, as the prime time for bone and muscle building is wasted away, which leads to obesity and cardiovascular diseases later in life. In the perspective of mental wellness, too much time spent on video games can build up stress and let them be vulnerable to toxic behaviours. One child, for example, can be subjected to verbal abuse by other players after a lost game - which exposes him or her to inappropriate language and even cyber-bullying. That is to say, games significantly hinder children's physiological and cognitive developments.

Beyond the drawbacks of unrestricted gaming, the benefits of outdoor play are substantial. Kids' childhoods will be definitely more fun, memorable and healthy if spent on outdoor pastimes. Kids taking up sports or participating in outdoor games with their peers gradually build up their stamina and endurance levels. Activities like tag or soccer strengthen their muscles and develop a fitness baseline. Moreover, communicating with their teammates while doing these recreational activities fosters social skills and a problem solving mindset. For instance, to build a sandcastle, children are encouraged to work together and split tasks to each individual - one person to collect water, another to dig a hole, etc. These cognitive attributes are essential to their adulthood, and children practicing these will be more likely to achieve success in the future.

In conclusion, playing computer games for long periods can be detrimental to children's physical and mental health. Engaging in outdoor activities, on the other hand, helps develop a healthy body and mind.
