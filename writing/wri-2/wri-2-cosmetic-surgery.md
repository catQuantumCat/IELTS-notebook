>**An increasing number of people choosing to have cosmetic surgery in order to improve**
**their appearance. Why do people have operations to change the way they look? Do you**  **think this is positive or negative development?**


Reason to go under a surgery:

It is said that more people opt for artificial changes through cosmetic surgery. The reason behind this trend, and my personal take on it, will be discussed in this essay.

People nowadays are willing to go under the knife to have their appearance altered. This can be because of a desire to be more attractive in the eyes of others. This is not a recent human trait, but is amplified in today's world due to the spread of social media platforms and TV commercials. For example, there are only a handful of supermodels all around the globe, but they are paid by fashion brands to set far-fetched beauty standards. Some wealthy individuals choose not to fight back this advertising scheme, but instead opt for cosmetic surgery to achieve those goals - to be stunning and elegant. Online posts and forums make information like this more wide-spread, turning a process once only spoken of in secret, now socially accepted. 

However - from my perspective - this development can do more harm than good. Firstly, to have this trend continue at an extreme level is to wrongly promote appearance attributes over other morals. An individual should be judged based on intrinsic qualities, like kindness, bravery or empathy, rather than physical appearance - a metric that social platforms prioritize. Secondly, low-cost operations for people with a tight budget can lead to detrimental, irreversible health consequences. By cutting cost and failing to recruit well-trained doctors, the clients' lives are at risk. Therefore, I think this is a negative movement.

In short, more people are choosing to have cosmetic surgery due to the rise of online platforms, personalised generated pictures and modern machinery. Yet it remains a worrying growth, for the people's awareness and health.
