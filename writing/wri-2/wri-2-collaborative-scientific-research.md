
Joint effort


>*Some people say that scientists worldwide should work together to do scientific research, but others worry this may cause problems.*
>
>*Discuss both views and give your opinion.*

It is said that scientific research should be conducted together between scholars, while others are afraid of the potential difficulties of doing it this way. While ownership and recognition recognision are among the problems, I personally support collaborating efforts in academic activities.

Some people might argue against working together in doing scientific research, that carrying out experiments with others enables some malicious academics to steal others' findings  - milestones that often take years to reach. For example, to be officially regarded as professors, Vietnamese scholars are required to have some number of papers published in international ~~pronounced~~ renowned scientific ~~magazines~~ journals. This incentivises those who want to be on the fast track to plagiarise experiments of which they are currently co-authors. It is possible for an individual to be praised and awarded, on the results that are only possible to have when working as a team.

Nevertheless, I'm strongly in favour of the view that executing experiments and identifying undiscovered facts should be done in a collaborative environment. This is an effective method to make the most use out of the currently available physical and human resources. Support between universities or nations make it possible for  regional colleges or third world countries to gain access to the most powerful computers and machines. In addition, for equipments that are enormous in size, agreements between countries are encouraged to have a part of those installed in their territories. In fact, the first image of the black hole ~~is~~ was taken thanks to the placement of multiple cameras across the EU. Moreover, joint efforts between scientists provide various respective and potential solutions to the problems at hand. This is crucial in detecting mistakes and biases early on in the process, which ensures objectively correct outputs. Because of those advantages, the cooperative nature of the academic activities is necessary and beneficial to both the scientific community, and to the general public. 

In short, collaborating in academic ~~researching~~ research allows people working in academic fields with shady intentions to earn benefits from the work of others. However, the benefits of shared equipment and quality assurance far outweigh the mentioned drawback.
