Nowadays many people choose to be self-employed, rather than to work for a company or organization.
Why might this be the case?
What could be the disadvantages of being self employed?




- Those who work for themselves
- Be one’s own boss
- Personal business owners
- Contractor / freelancer


In the current society, a growing number of individuals are nowadays opting for self-employment over traditional corporate jobs. This essay will discuss the compelling reasons behind this trend, as well as examining some potential drawbacks that might deter people from this form of working.

To start, the main notion that hooks people into being their own boss lies in it's innate properties - time flexibility. Unlike employees who spend one-third of their day in office cubicles, freelancers have the ability to dynamically choose their own working hours. Based on the clients' requirements and their own schedule, one can focus on clearing tasks while others have fallen asleep, and hit the hay in the middle of a workday. Such wide selection of time makes achieving work-life balance more probable, especially when compared with the fixed business hours and the expectation from employers of working overtime. Creative freedom is also one of the characteristic aspect of self-employment. Personal business owners can have complete control on how the final product is, with tight control throughout the process of analysing, brainstorming, producing and refining the outcomes. All of that can be done without the corporate's capital interest  in mind. This unique prospect enables individuals to proactively pick projects that excites them and aligns with their core values.

Despite the mentioned benefits of doing freelance work; it might not be suitable for everyone out there. Many people still prefer contracted work for financial stability. For those who work by themselves - especially ones who are just starting out, it might a burden to find those who are in need of freelancers without strong networking in the industry. This, in turn, means the possibility of months with little to no income at all. The flexibility of this line of work can also be a double-edge sword for those with a lack of self-discipline. Time management and task planning, which are the skills that are highly needed for a productive work day, are even more crucial for those who independently runs their own business. Failure to obtain those skills can be detrimental to their personal career.

In conclusion, people choose to build their personal business for its personalized working hours and artistic freedom. However, the uncertainty of income and the high requirement of self-control makes company jobs a viable choice.
