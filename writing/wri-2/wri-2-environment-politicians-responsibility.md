It is said that the government should anticipate more in the prevention of and solution to environmental challenges, as actions from individuals are not as effective. While the effects that the latter have on climate transformation are often underappreciated, I do agree for politicians to be more responsible in this matter. 

On the one hand, while the public do not have an extensive amount of resources, they can contribute to this promising movement of reverting climate change through various ways. One of them is to use public transportation - like buses, trams or subways. Being one of the largest greenhouse gases contributors, it is only beneficial for cars to be less frequently on the road. Additionally, buyers can also choose steal water bottles, second-hand clothing or refurbished phones over plastic cups, fast-fashion items and new devices. This new kind of sustainable consumerism helps to reduce tonnes of waste each year. That is to say, each environmentally conscious choice made by the public can yield significant outputs to save our planet. 

On the other hand, I do believe that with great power and trust comes great responsiblities. As such, politicians should do everything in their power, utilise every resources and give realistic directives as a way to restore the ecological status. In particular, by subsidising public transport cost or allocating budget for R&D in road infrastructure, legislators can - and should - make the public transportation system more accessible and appealing to their residents. The officials are also responsible not only for making international commitment, but also for putting them into practice. Viet Nam throughout the years has become the spotlight in this aspect, as the government has shown efforts in reaching the neutral carbon goal issued by the UN before 2050, by promoting electric vehicles and investing in eco-friendly energy sources.

In short, as lawmakers have more resources in hand, they should participate in solving crucial ecological problems. Yet it should be a joint effort from them and their citizens to help putting a stop to global warming - among other environmental problems.
