It is claimed that the governments should not take up the responsibility of sponsoring university programs, due to the lack of possibility and potential benefits of doing so. I firmly believe in the opposite view - that providing free undergraduate courses is not only something that nations can do, but also an achievement that brings economic values.

It is a common fact that undergrad education requires significantly more capital investments from students. Yet this is not to say that providing a nationwide free college education is impossible to execute. In today's world of broadband Internet, one solution is to move the teaching itself online - removing the physical limits. For majors that need real-life lectures, favourable policies and salaries are needed to keep academic talents working in domestic instituions. Some of the developed countries in Europe have applied those approaches, proving that it is not only viable but also sustainable. Combining with a portion of taxation redirected for scholarships and support from NGOs, this shows viability in this attempt from the government.

This long-term investment in the young generation can in turn have positive impacts on the government's growth. Making undergrad programs free for all young adults gives students the freedom to be proactive in their future path. This creates diversity in the national workforce, with employees from many backgrounds being equipped with a strong knowledge base. These talented workers can bring productivity, creativity and profitability to the private and public sectors, and also to the country through increased taxation. Moreover, the goverment can use this outcome to attract more foreign companies to pour money into the local market, which also contributes to its financial growth. Putting education on the same priority level as economic growth and military means that the national welfare is ensured.

In conclusion, the inclusive vision of making university courses accessible without cost to the future generation is not only feasible, but also beneficial for the stakeholders. Students will be encouraged to choose their own path based on suitability, while nations will have their young demographic well-educated.
