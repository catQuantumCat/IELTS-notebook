## Why?

Some nations in the world have already applied limitations to the hours people can work in a day. As there are many ways to explain these decisions, I'm in favour of these positive changes.

From my point of view, the main reason behind this promising trend is that nations nowadays can afford to do so. Thanks to the advancement in bleeding-edge technologies - like AI or humanoid robots, citizens are no longer needed to work around the clock to make ends meet. Because of that, and to ensure the national welfare, governments are taking better care of their citizens - with limiting working hours being one of the initiatives. Moreover, many studies have concluded that working in a degraded state of mind can have negative effects on productivity. Thus, lawmakers are encouraged to make sure that their citizens are well-rested before their next working shifts. 

As there are many malicious companies forcing their workers to work overtime, ~~sometime~~ sometimes without ~~compensations~~ compensation, having rest sessions protected by the law is a welcoming change. Employees are no longer obligated to stay at work longer than they should be. Instead, they can use that valuable time for their friends, family and ~~themself~~ themselves. By participating in physical activities, or joining clubs in their free time, these activities can effectively recharge their battery. At a higher level, ~~there~~ these approaches help to protect the people's health by not overworking. This is crucial for an optimistic population, encouraging them to have a more joyful lifestyle. Additionally, having their people feel happy and engerized can facilitate the domestic market, as they often spend more money when in a good mood.

In short, legislation that limits time spent working brings nothing but advantages. As people no longer need to work long hours to earn an income, this benefits their psychological states and the nation's financial status.
