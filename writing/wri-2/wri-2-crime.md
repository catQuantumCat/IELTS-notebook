
> In some countries, a high proportion of criminal acts are committed by teenagers. Why has this happened? What can be done to deal with this?


It is said that more and more offences are being committed by **juveniles**. There are some leading causes to this trend, as well as the potential solutions, which will be discussed in this essay.

*The heart of the issue lies* in the fact that some youngsters are not cared for or educated properly. Parents nowadays are so busy working round the clock to make ends meet, they neglect giving attention to their children. Some teens born into low-income families even drop out from school to cope with the financial difficulties. Thus, they are not fully aware of the consequences of their actions, which brings them closer to social evils. Additionally, people of this age are vulnerable to improper guidance on the topic of money. This can create a distorted view, that doing unlawful activities is the way to make a living. Without timely precautions, minor crimes - pickpocketing travellers or stealing from their peers - can set in motion a series of criminal acts as they grow older.

To combat this, a joint effort between family and government must be made. Parents need to make time for their children, to understand and share the problems they are facing. This emotional support is beneficial in all aspects, especially in preventing potential rebellious behaviours. Meanwhile, the governments need to ensure that their young citizens' priority is to learn and grow up as a decent human being, and not resort to crime to earn money. This can be done by giving financial aid and tax reduction for unprivileged families, and scholarships for their children. All of these should be done, to ensure a bright future for the young generation, and to lower the juvenile crime rate.

In conclusion, youngsters are more likely to break the law if they are not given the opportunity to be well educated. Thereby, it is the parent and official authority's responsibility to help to deter illegal activities done by this age group.
