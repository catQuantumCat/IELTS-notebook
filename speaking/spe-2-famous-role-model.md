From my point of view, the one well-known person that will have a positive impacts on other people, especially youngsters and young adults would be professor David J Malan. He's a professor at Harvard University, teaching computer science and also he is the goto person on the internet when it comes to explaining technologies definitions.

In the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, as I was surfing on YouTube, I came across one of his video explaining about the C programming ,and I was blown away by his approach on how to grasp the fundamental features of this language, like using metaphors and analogies to help student understand memory allocation and pointers, features that at that time were not comprehensively addressed on other places on the internet.

As a CS professor, he turned CS50 - the introductory course to computer science from a already top-of-the-class course to become a high production value, beginner friendly series  on YouTube, which is watched by millions of students from different path of life all over the world. He is also seen on other videos where he tried to explain CS terminologies for people with various knowledge of this field.

His expressive way of helping collage students to understand the basic notions in the world of software development exemplify the fact that he is deeply passionate about what he is doing Also, by using visual examples - like the good-old phone book to show how effective a binary search is - think outside the box -> unique solutions to problems.

Prof. David J. Malan has inspired me to become a software developer myself, and I personally think that his commitment to teach CS differently would be an inspiration to young adults.
