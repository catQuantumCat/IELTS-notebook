>*Describe something that was broken in your home and then repaired*
>**You should say:**
>- **What it is**
>- **How it was broken**
>- **How you got it repaired**

A few years back, my family had a home PC. It was useful for all of the family members - my dad used it for online blog reading, my mum utilized it to take mock tests on her driving license, and I loved playing pre-installed video games on it as a kid.

One morning, my mum just starting her learning session for the day, she pressed the power button, but there was no response. The screen remained black, while the usual noise of the hard disk booting up was replaced by silence. We did the usual troubleshooting - checking the power cord, the display port or the infamous turning it off and on again, but still, the computer remained unresponsive.

I wasn't familiar with computers back then, and my parents didn't want to risk further breaking it by blindly open the processing unit, so we called an IT technician. After a brief moment of checking, he told us that the computer had not been regularly inspected. Layers of dust blocked the exhaust fans, preventing the hot air to escape, which caused the entire hardware system to overheat. A thermal paste replacement with some internal cleaning brought it back to life, and fortunately it did not cost much.

We were relived, since replacing the whole computer would cost us an arm and a leg. This incident taught me about proper maintenance for the household electronic.
