### What kind of people do you usually see in the news?
- The individual who make appearances on mass media are often those who make big impacts to our daily life.
- It's both beneficial for the audiences, who always want to be updated with the latest changes made by the mentioned people, and for the media publishers, who can increase their click through rate and ad revenue by providing the hottest news about those who make significant changes.
- Streotypical figures might be politicians whose decisions directly impact the daily life, or inspritional figures like fundraiser with positive contributions to the society.
- Stereotypical figures: criminals, politicians, inspirational figures 
- Bad societal influences
### Are stories about celebrities in the news always true?
I would argue that not all articles reflect truthfully about famous people's professional and personal life. 
- This might be because as negative news make bigger splash than positive news, some media outlets would intentionally fabricate parts of a celebrety's action or scandal to attract more eye balls, and thus more advertisement income.
- The stories might be revolve around the star's daily life, their love relationships or their upcoming projects.
- Alternatively, some personalities, can by themself, portray unrealistic images of their public life, as a way to attracts people or to shape media narratives to suit their interests.
- Stories that revolve around celebrities’ lives
- Shape media narratives to suit their interests
- Engage in bad behavior
- Ramifications (consequences) of promoting a false sense of self: fans try to chase unrealistic expectations for their lives, lose their sense of self, never feel sufficient or good enough, lose self-confidence
    

### Is news often about famous people? Why?
- I would say that it's depends on the type of target audience and the style of writing of each news outlet. 
- often more than not, news _does_ feature famous people mainly because their lives and actions tend to capture widespread public interest. This inherent interest can drive viewership, making it a commercially viable strategy for many news outlets. Furthermore, the activities of influencers can sometimes intersect with broader societal events, providing a relatable point of view.
