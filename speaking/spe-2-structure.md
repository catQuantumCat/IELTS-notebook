## About the cue card
- Don't trust it wholly
- No repetition between task 1 and task 2 in a same test
- Vocabs are reusable

## One min prep strategy
1. Choose topic/content that is easy to answer
	- Have lots of features
	- Expandable (adding more features when the time did not end)
	- Easy to make up content - lies
	- Ignore the prompt's adj
	- Ignore the 1st idea
2. Write down as many <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">**uncommon vocabs**</mark> as possible that are related to the prompt  - don't choose those that can be used for many other topics
3. Grammar point: what tense to use
![[spe-2-structure-prep-sample.png]]

## The fluency strategy
Spit the talk into 4 suggestions from the cue card - use each of them as an opening points for the 4 phrases of the talk.

Per each point of 4, focus more on the 3 criteria: Fluency, Vocab and Grammar

![[spe-doc-mat-clark.pdf#page=102]]

- Avoid changing/paraphrasing too much the suggestions: risking making grammar mistakes (slight changes are fine)


### Walk-through - the fluency strategy
>[!NOTE]
>- No more than 40s per each prompt

### Introduction/first point prep
<mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">This should be short, simple and without much of the content</mark> - simply to lay out the subject, context.
1. Filler first sentence
2. A linking to actual content/subject
3. The subject itself
4. (optional) additional info
	- The additional info should starts with linking word: "where, who, in fact, although, particularly"
> *<mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">Okey then, I should start off by answering who the person is</mark> (1), and I think I would probably select(2) Mr.A (3), who is the CEO of company B (4)',...*
#### First point content
#### Second point prep
Again:
1. A filler sentence
2. The second prompt
3. A linking sentence
4. The actual (opening) content

> *<mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">Now concerning the matter of</mark> (1) why he is famous (2) and what I have to mention here is that (3) he is one of the founding father of the US (4)*
#### Second point 
- The content should be supported by some 1 - 2 facts.
#### Third point prep
1. A filler sentence
2. The third prompt
3. A linking sentence
4. The actual content of the prompt

> *<mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">Moving onto the area of</mark> (1) what impacts does he have on the society (2), you really have to understand that as a politician (3), he has the absolute power to be a dictator(4).*

#### Third point 
#### (optional) Fourth point prep
The time might not be available for this fourth point, but it's alright, since the prompts are only suggestions

> <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">To end, I would like to bring in the point of</mark> what I would to if I was given a chance to meet him.


### Example: type of animal
Okey then, when it comes to this topic, the one animal that I would definitely choose is cat, which is one of human's most adorable and closest best friends.

**As portrayed** in cartoons and pop culture products, cats can be found anywhere - maybe in one's home - most often sleeping, or on the street, or even in some specialty coffee shops - where their appearances and cuteness can be one of the main selling points. For the more unfortunate ones, it's the adoption centres, or hidden spaces in public parks the place they call home.

I have to mention that <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">not only have I witnessed a ridiculous amount of cats, but I myself also</mark> have a feline friend at home to cuddle - she's calico breed. It was actually 6 when I first encountered with a cat, when my mom's friend brought over an orange cat that kept me companied that entire afternoon.

To end, I would like to bring in the point regarding the attitude of most people toward cats in general, and to that I would say that most individuals (or those that I have met) love cats. In many cultures cats are considered as lucky animals - mostly in Asian countries, where cat-shaped statues are sculptured and placed at the entrances with a hope of bringing in businesses.
